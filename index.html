Biography

I am currently a PhD student in the GAIA team of GIPSA lab in Grenoble, under the supervision of Prof. Romain Couillet and of Nicolas Tremblay. My work focuses on the improvement and theoretical understanding of unsupervised graph spectral clustering and the methodsare at the frontier between graph signal processing, statistical physics, computer science and random matrix theory.

This interdisciplinary background allows me to see different angles giving me a versatile approach to problem-solving.

Optimal Laplacian regularization for sparse spectral community detection

In this article we provide a new interpretation on why regularization helps in sparse community detection as well as providing results on the optimal regularization to be adopted.

A unified framework for spectral clustering in sparse graphs

In this article we show how the benchmark spectral clustering techniques can all be explained under the same, elegant framework. We further provide a highly performing algorithm for community detection.
