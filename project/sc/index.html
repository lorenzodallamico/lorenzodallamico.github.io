Spectral methods for graph clustering

Jan 1, 0001

Network theory is of central importance in interdisciplinary science. The ability of recovering properties of items starting from their interaction has key applications in many fields, ranging from biology, to social science, passing through electronics and Physics. Clustering is the ability to group the items of a system in categories sharing similar properties. The spectral techinques represent a fast solution to this problem. Solving this problem for sparse and heterogenous networks is of utmost importance to address the problem on real graphs.
